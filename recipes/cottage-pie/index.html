Cottage Pie

Ground beef and veggies in gravy baked with mashed potatoes on top

This recipe works best cooked in a 12 inch cast iron skillet. If that is not available another oven safe skillet can be used, or transfer from skillet to another oven safe dish before topping with potatoes.

A lot of instructions in this recipe are not exact, so taste frequently.

Pairs well with the rest of the red wine not used in the recipe.

The Bachan’s sauce can be substituted for Worcestershire sauce if needed. The idea to use Bachan’s Japanese barbecue sauce instead of the more traditional Worcestershire sauce comes from Bachan’s recipe. I also took inspiration from Tastes Better From Scratch for instructions on how to make a traditional cottage pie.

Ingredients

  • Meat
    • 1 pound of high fat ground beef
  • Produce
    • 1 yellow onion
    • 3 ribs of celery
    • 2 large carrots
    • 4 cloves garlic
    • 2 & 1/2 pounds of russet potatoes (about 4 large potatoes)
    • Green onion for topping
  • Eggs & Dairy
    • 1/2 cup sour cream
    • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter at room temperature
  • Pantry
    • 2 cups beef broth
    • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
    • Bachan's Japanese Barbecue Sauce
    • Can of fried onions for topping
  • Freezer
    • Frozen corn
    • Frozen peas
  • Alcohol
    • 1/4 cup of red wine
  • Spices & Seasonings
    • Dried thyme
    • Dried rosemary
  • Staples
    • Flour
    • Salt
    • Pepper

Instructions

  • Prepare Mashed Potatoes
    • Peel and cut potatoes into small even pieces and start boiling. Move on to next step while boiling keeping an eye on the potatoes to avoid overcooking. Drain when done.
  • Preparation
    • Dice the onion, peeled carrots, and celery.
    • Thinly slice the green onions.
    • Mince the garlic.
    • Preheat oven to 350 degrees F.
  • Cook Beef and Vegetables
    • Brown beef in the cast iron skillet.
    • Remove beef and set aside keeping fat in pan.
    • Add onions, carrots, and celery and cook until softened.
    • Add garlic and cook 1 to 2 minutes until fragrant.
  • Make Gravy
    • Add tomato paste and cook until darkened.
    • Deglaze with wine.
    • Add in broth and cooked beef.
    • Lower heat to maintain a gentle simmer.
    • Add flour a bit at a time to thicken. Keep in mind it will continue to thicken as it simmers. Should be about 1/4 cup of flour added.
    • Simmer until gravy is thick.
    • Add in about 4 tablespoons of the Bachan’s barbecue sauce until desired sweetness and taste is reached. Add extra salt if needed keeping in mind the Bachan’s sauce is very salty.
    • Stir in rosemary and thyme to taste.
  • Make the Pie
    • Add in frozen peas and corn until desired ratio.
    • Mash potatoes with sour cream and butter. Gently warm if needed to melt in butter. Add salt and pepper to taste.
    • Remove from heat and spread potatoes over the top. Make sure to spread evenly and get all the way to the edge.
    • Bake for 25 to 35 minutes until the potatoes have reached desired browning.
  • Serve
    • Let cool for a few minutes before serving.
    • Top with green onions and fried onions.
